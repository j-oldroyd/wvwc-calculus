Definition 14.2.1. Scalar line lntegral.

Suppose that \(C\) is a smooth curve in \(\RR^{2}\) and that the function \(f(x,y)\) is continuous on \(C\text{.}\) The scalar line integral of \(f\) along \(C\) is the number \(\int_{C}f(x,y)\dd{s}\) defined to be
\begin{equation*} \int_{C}f(x,y)\dd{s} = \lim_{\Delta s\to 0}\sum_{j=1}^{k}f(x_{j},y_{j})\Delta s\text{.} \end{equation*}
