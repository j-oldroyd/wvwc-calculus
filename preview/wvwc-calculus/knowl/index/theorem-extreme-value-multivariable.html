Theorem 12.7.7. Extreme Value Theorem.

Let \(f(x,y)\) be a continuous function defined on a closed and bounded set \(D\text{.}\)
 10 
This means that \(D\) is finite in size and that it includes its boundary.
Then \(f\) is guaranteed to have an absolute maximum and absolute minimum. Furthermore, these must occur either at a critical point or on the boundary of \(D\text{.}\)
