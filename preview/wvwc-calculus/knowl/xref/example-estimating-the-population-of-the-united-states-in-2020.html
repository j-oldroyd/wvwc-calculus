Example 3.7.4. Estimating the population of the United States in 2020.

Use the differential equation from Example 3.5.4 and the fact that the current population of the US is about 323.1 million to estimate the population of the US in 2020.
Solution.
Recall that \(P(t)\) in Example 3.5.4 represented the population of the US (in millions) \(t\) years after 1990. To estimate the population in 2020, we want to find the linearization of \(P\) at \(t = 27\text{.}\) This is given by
\begin{equation*} L(t) = P(27) + P'(27)(t - 27). \end{equation*}
Since \(P(27) = 323.1\) and
\begin{equation*} P^\prime(27) = \frac{1}{23500}P(532-P) = \frac{1}{23500}(323.1)(532-323.1) = 2.9, \end{equation*}
we have
\begin{equation*} L(t) = 323.1 + 2.9(t-27). \end{equation*}
Hence the population of the US in 2020 should be
\begin{equation*} L(30) = 323.1 + 2.9\cdot3 = 331.8, \end{equation*}
or about 331.8 million people.
