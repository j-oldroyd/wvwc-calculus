Example 13.4.2. Center of mass of an annulus.

Find the center of mass of the plate \(D\) outside of the circle \(x^{2}+y^{2} = 1\) and inside the circle \(x^{2} + y^{2} = 4\text{,}\) with density \(\rho(x,y) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2} + y^{2}}}\text{.}\)
🔗
Solution.
The mass is given by
\begin{align*} \iint_{D} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}}}\,dA \amp = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{1}^{2}\frac{r}{r}\,dr\,d\theta \\ \amp = 2\pi \end{align*}
The moments are
\begin{align*} M_{x} \amp = \iint_{D}\frac{y}{\sqrt{x^{2} + y^{2}}}\,dA \\ \amp = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{1}^{2} \frac{r\sin\theta}{r}r\,dr\,d\theta \\ \amp = 0 \end{align*}
and likewise \(M_{y} = 0\text{.}\) So the center of mass is \((0,0)\text{.}\)
