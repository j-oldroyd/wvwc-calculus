Theorem 11.5.7. Vector and Scalar Equations of a Plane.

If \(\mathbf{r}_{0} = \dotprod{x_{0},y_{0},z_{0}}\) is the position vector for some point on a plane with normal vector \(\mathbf{n} = \dotprod{a,b,c}\text{,}\) then the position vector \(\mathbf{r} = \dotprod{x,y,z}\) of any other point on the plane must satisfy the vector equation of the plane:
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{n}\cdot(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}_{0}) = 0. \end{equation*}
Equivalently, any point \((x,y,z)\) must also satisfy the equivalent scalar equation of the plane:
\begin{equation*} a(x-x_{0})+b(y-y_{0})+c(z-z_{0}) = 0. \end{equation*}
