Example 13.8.2. The Jacobian for polar coordinates.

Determine \(J(r,\theta)\) for the coordinate transformation from polar coordinates to Cartesian coordinates.
🔗
Solution.
The coordinate transformation here is given by
\begin{equation*} x = r\cos(\theta)\text{ and }y = r\sin(\theta)\text{.} \end{equation*}
We have
\begin{align*} x_{r} \amp = \cos\theta \\ x_{\theta} \amp = -r\sin\theta \\ y_{r} \amp = \sin\theta \\ y_{\theta} \amp = r\cos\theta \text{.} \end{align*}
Hence,
\begin{equation*} J(r,\theta) = r\cos^{2}\theta + r\sin^{2}\theta = r\text{.} \end{equation*}
