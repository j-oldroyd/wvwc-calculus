Example 3.1.1. Secant lines on a parabola.

Let \(f(x) = x^{2}\text{.}\) Find the slope of the secant line through \((2,f(2))\) and \((x,f(x))\text{.}\)
Solution.
Since the slope of the secant line is the average rate of change, we get that the slope must be equal to
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(x) - f(2)}{x-2} = \frac{x^{2} - 4}{x-2} = x+2. \end{equation*}
