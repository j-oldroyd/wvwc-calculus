Example 13.3.5. Computing the volume of a sphere.

Find the volume of a sphere of radius \(\rho\text{.}\)
🔗
Solution.
First, we can center the sphere at the origin without loss of generality. Such a sphere is given by \(x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2} = \rho^{2}\text{.}\) If we solve for \(z\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} z = \pm\sqrt{\rho^{2} - x^{2} - y^{2}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Let \(D\) denote the disk of radius \(\rho\) in the \(xy\)-plane centered at the origin. Then the volume of the sphere is
\begin{align*} 2\iint_{D}\sqrt{\rho^{2} - x^{2} - y^{2}}\,dA \amp = 2\int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{\rho}\sqrt{\rho^{2} - r^{2}}r\,dr\,d\theta\\ \amp = -\int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{\rho^{2}}^{0}\sqrt{u}\,du\,d\theta\\ \amp = \frac{2}{3}\int_{0}^{2\pi}u^{3/2}\bigg]_{u=0}^{\rho^{2}}\,d\theta\\ \amp = \frac{4\pi}{3}\rho^{3} \end{align*}
