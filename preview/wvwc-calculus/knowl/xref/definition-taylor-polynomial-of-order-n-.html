Definition 9.7.1. Taylor Polynomial of Order \(n\).

Let \(f(x)\) be a function that is \(n\)-times differentiable at \(x=a\) (so that \(f(a), f^\prime(a),\ldots,f^{(n)}(a)\) all exist). The corresponding Taylor polynomial of order \(n\) centered at \(x=a\) is given by
\begin{equation*} T_n(x) = \sum_{k=0}^n \frac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!}(x-a)^k\text{.} \end{equation*}
