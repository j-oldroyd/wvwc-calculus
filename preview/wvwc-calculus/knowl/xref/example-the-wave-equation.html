Example 12.3.6. The Wave Equation.

One of the most important PDEs is the wave equation
\begin{equation*} u_{tt} = c^{2}u_{xx}, \end{equation*}
which is used to model the displacement of a string \(u(x,t)\) at position \(x\) and time \(t\text{.}\) Determine if
\begin{equation*} u(x,t) = \sin(x-3t) + \ln(x+3t) \end{equation*}
is a solution of the wave equation.
ðŸ”—
Solution.
We need to plug \(u\) into the PDE and check that it satisfies the PDE. Since
\begin{align*} u_{tt} & = -9\cos(x-3t) - \frac{9}{(x+3t)^{2}} \\ u_{xx} & = -\sin(x-3t) - \frac{1}{(x+3t)^{2}} \end{align*}
it follows that \(u_{tt} = 9u_{xx}\text{.}\) So \(u(x,t)\) satisfies the wave equation with \(c=3\text{,}\) and so is a solution of the wave equation.
