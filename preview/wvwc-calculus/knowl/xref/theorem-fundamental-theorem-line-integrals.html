Theorem 14.3.4. Fundamental theorem of line integrals.

Suppose that \(\vb{F}\) is a conservative vector field (in the sense of DefinitionÂ 14.3.1) with continuous components on an open simply connected region \(D\text{.}\) Then \(\vb{F}\) is path independent. Furthermore, if \(\vb{F} = \grad f\) and \(C\) is a curve in \(D\) with initial point \(A\) and terminal point \(B\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \int_{C}\vb{F}\cdot d\vb{r} = f(B) - f(A)\text{.} \end{equation*}
