Theorem 9.7.10. Taylor’s Theorem (Lagrange’s form).

Let \(f(x)\) be a function that is \((n+1)\)-times differentiable. Let \(T_n(x)\) denote the corresponding Taylor polynomial of order \(n\) centered at \(a\text{.}\) Then there exists \(z\) between \(a\) and \(x\) such that
\begin{equation*} f(x) = T_n(x) + \frac{f^{(k+1)}(z)}{(k+1)!}(x-a)^{k+1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
