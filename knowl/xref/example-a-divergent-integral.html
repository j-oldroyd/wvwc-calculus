Example 7.4.2. A Divergent Integral.

Determine if \(\int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{x}\,dx\) is convergent or divergent.
Solution.
By definition, we have
\begin{align*} \int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{x}\,dx & = \lim_{b\to\infty}\int_{1}^{b}\frac{1}{x}\,dx \\ & = \lim_{b\to\infty}(\ln b - \ln 1) \\ & = \infty. \end{align*}
Hence the integral is divergent.
